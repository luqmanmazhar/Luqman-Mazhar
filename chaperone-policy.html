Chaperone Policy

Primus Physio — last updated May 2026

Your right to a chaperone: Any patient at Primus Physio has the right to request a chaperone to be present during their physiotherapy assessment or treatment. This right will always be respected.

What Is a Chaperone?

A chaperone is a person — usually a trusted friend, family member or carer — who is present during a clinical examination or treatment. Their role is to provide support and reassurance to the patient and to ensure transparency in the clinical encounter.

Who May Request a Chaperone?

Any patient may request a chaperone at any time, for any reason. You do not need to explain your reasons for requesting one. Children and young people under 18 should normally be accompanied by a parent or guardian unless they specifically request otherwise and the clinician is satisfied this is appropriate.

How to Request a Chaperone

  • You can request a chaperone when booking your appointment
  • You can request one at the start of your session
  • You can request one at any point during your session

If a chaperone is requested and cannot be accommodated at that session (for example if you have not brought someone with you), your appointment may be rescheduled so that a chaperone can be present.

Intimate Examinations

Where an assessment or treatment involves examination of intimate areas of the body, the clinical rationale will be explained clearly beforehand and your consent will be obtained. You have the right to refuse any aspect of examination or treatment at any time. A chaperone will always be offered for intimate examinations.

Practitioner Responsibility

Luqman Mazhar, as the treating physiotherapist, is responsible for ensuring:

  • Patients are informed of their right to a chaperone
  • All requests for a chaperone are facilitated wherever possible
  • The presence or absence of a chaperone is documented in the clinical record
  • Treatment is conducted with professionalism, dignity and respect at all times
